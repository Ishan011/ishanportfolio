Taptrack

Hypercasual mobile-based game made on Unity 3D.

Introduction

Taptrack is a hypercasual mobile based game which offers quite a naive gameplay, yet very addicting for people playing it. The aim here is to design a minimalistic user-interface and plan ways to have good user engagement and monetisation models in terms of advertisements as its future goals.

Process

UX Research

To better analyse and get an understanding of hypercasual games, secondary research was used and the findings were as follows.

Monetisation

In-app advertising model in form of banner ads and videos, rewarding gamers with in-game currency that can be utilised in buying exciting addons being updated regularly in shop.

User Engagement

To maintain engagement levels, players could be rewarded with daily coins. Additionally, allowing users to share and challenge their friends. Furthermore, leaderboard could be implemented to promote healthy competition using google play games.

Game Assets

Used Figma to create UI designs and later exported them on Unity to develop this game.

Reflection

I certainly got more closer to what I envisioned for this game when I tried to make this during my undergraduate. I was able to utilise both my UX knowledge and my computers science background to make this project a success for me.

This project allowed me to explore and learn basics in Unity. I wish to use google play services later to have my vision turn into reality in terms of user engagement and monetisation models.
