Taptrack

Hypercasual mobile-based game made on Unity 3D.

Introduction

Taptrack is a hypercasual mobile based game which offers quite a naive gameplay, yet a addicting one. I have worked on this project earlier on during my undergraduate and through this module I wish to refine it further.

The aim here is to develop and design a minimalistic user-interface, moreover plan the ways to have a good user engagement and the ways to monetise this mobile application as its future goals.

Process

UX Research

To better analyse and get an understanding of hypercasual games, secondary research was used and the findings were as follows.

Monetisation

In-app advertising model in form of banner ads and videos, rewarding gamers with in-game currency that can be utilised in buying exciting addons being updated regularly in shop.

User Engagement

To maintain engagement levels, players could be rewarded with daily coins. Additionally, allowing users to share and challenge their friends. Furthermore, leaderboard could be implemented to promote healthy competition using google play games.

Game Assets

Figma was utilised to create UI designs and assets which were later exported on Unity game engine while developing this game.

Reflection

This project allowed me to get myself familiar with the interface and understanding the basics of Unity game engine. I got to utilise a decent mix of some of my skills that I have learned so far. Since, I have a computers science background, I was able to make sense of the codebase and make tweaks around to match it to my needs. Moreover, I used my knowledge about user experience and user-interface designing while planing and creating assets for this game.

Reflecting on the work that I have done in this module, I certainly have got more closer to what I had envisioned for this game back during my undergraduate.

In terms of user-interface, I was not able to develop in-game shop exactly in a manner that I had planed and designed on Figma. So that is something that I would need to work further on. Furthermore, next goals would be to integrate google play services for better user engagement and to monetise this application.
